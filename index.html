  • Taking the land by means of Conquest
  • Trading for land

Those that resisted were either killed in war, or jailed.

Act for the Government and Protection of Indians: 1850

One of the first laws passed by the newly formed California state legislature. It allowed Native Americans to be subjected to any vagrency charges based "on the complaint of any reasonable citizen".

Many Indians were incarcerated as a result and were often found working on the county chain gang.

Southern Redemption: 1877

A movement to return the U.S. south back to its pre-civil war system of labor. Several state and local laws were passed that could accomplish this:

  • Contract-Enforcement Laws: workers cannot break a labor contract.
  • Vagrancy Laws
  • Convict-Lease System: private parties can buy prisoner labor.
